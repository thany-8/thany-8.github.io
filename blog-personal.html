Building Long-Lasting Connections

I value relationships that continue beyond one class, project, job, or event. Through school, tutoring, community work, and technology programs, I have met people who have supported my growth and encouraged me to keep moving forward.

I try to give that same support to others. I prefer having a smaller circle of meaningful connections over collecting contacts I barely know. To me, a strong connection is built through trust, consistency, and genuinely caring about another person's journey.

Leaving a Positive Impression

I want people to feel respected, supported, and comfortable when they interact with me. I pay attention when someone shares something about their life, studies, or goals, and I try to remember it and follow up later.

Whether I am helping a student find a resource, explaining a calculus problem, or working with a teammate on a project, I try to be dependable and helpful. I do not follow a special formula—I simply believe that small, thoughtful actions can make people feel seen and valued.

Mindful Communication

I am naturally more of a listener than the loudest person in the room. I like to understand what someone is saying before I respond, especially when working through a problem or collaborating with a team.

Being thoughtful with my words has helped me build trust, communicate clearly, and learn from people with different experiences. I may be quiet at first, but when I speak, I want my words to be honest, useful, and meaningful.
